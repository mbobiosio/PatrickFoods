Description

INTRODUCTION Cocoa, the main ingredient in chocolate production continues to experience high demands across the globe. A key cash crop, it is Nigeria’s highest earning non-oil export with 2017 cocoa earnings put at over $700 million (₦250 billion). THE OPPORTUNITY The Agropartnerships Cocoa export trade accords you the opportunity to collaborate and profit from the export of high quality dried cocoa beans to chocolate processors in The Netherlands. You simply purchase the number of units you would like to trade while we facilitate the sourcing, processing, logistics and sales. The entire process cycle from sourcing to sales and returns is approximated at 4 months after which partners get a return of capital and profit of 16%. INTERESTING FACTS ✔Nigeria is the 4th largest producer of Cocoa in the world. ✔70% of the world’s Cocoa is produced in West African countries - Ivory Coast, Ghana, Nigeria and Cameroon. ✔One Cocoa tree produces an average of 60 pods annually with each pod containing about 40 Cocoa beans each. That makes about 2400 beans which is enough to produce fifty 100g bars of chocolate. ✔Cocoa is used in making chocolate, cosmetic products, liquor, animal feed and other derivatives. USE CASE/PROCESSES We source ripe Cocoa pods from selected smallholder farmers in communities in Ondo, Edo, Oyo and Cross River states. The pods are opened and allowed to ferment before going through a flash dryer. After drying, a grading process is followed which ensures only the best quality Cocoa beans are exported. We package in 50kg branded jute bags which are loaded into containers before undergoing shipment to The Netherlands. The Cocoa beans is used in making chocolates and a host of other by products. COST COMPONENTS Sourcing, processing and packaging: 66% Logistics, freight, insurance, tariffs: 34% FINANCE & RETURNS One unit costs ₦90,000. Returns per unit: 16% 8 units represent 1 ton of Cocoa. Returns are calculated by subtracting the projected costs from the predetermined sale price and splitting the difference between the partner, the platform and the trade facilitator at a ratio of 50:20:30 respectively.
